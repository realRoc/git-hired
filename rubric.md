# git-hired Evaluation Rubric

This rubric is public so candidates, evaluators, and contributors can see what `git-hired` is trying to measure.

Use it as a calibration guide, not as a rigid hiring machine. Evidence quality matters before score precision.

## Score Scale

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | weak or missing evidence |
| 2 | some signal, but inconsistent or mostly self-reported |
| 3 | usable evidence with clear strengths and gaps |
| 4 | strong evidence across multiple work traces |
| 5 | standout evidence under realistic constraints |

## Core Dimensions

### 1. Agency

Can the candidate move without being told every step?

- 1: waits for complete instructions; gives up when ambiguous
- 3: can move a bounded task with reasonable direction
- 5: converts ambiguity into a plan, acts, checks, and adapts

### 2. AI Fluency

Does the candidate use agents as work partners, not just autocomplete?

- 1: uses AI for surface drafting or generic answers
- 3: can ask agents to inspect, compare, rewrite, and debug
- 5: orchestrates agents across specs, files, evidence, verification, and iteration

### 3. Product Sense

Does the candidate connect work to user value and business reality?

- 1: optimizes local output without user or metric context
- 3: understands user impact and makes reasonable tradeoffs
- 5: consistently ties decisions to users, constraints, metrics, and timing

### 4. Debugging Maturity

Does the candidate investigate instead of guessing?

- 1: retries blindly or blames tools
- 3: gathers symptoms, narrows causes, and verifies fixes
- 5: creates repeatable diagnosis loops and prevents regressions

### 5. Taste

Does the candidate care about clarity, simplicity, and quality?

- 1: accepts noisy or confusing output
- 3: improves copy, structure, and implementation quality when prompted
- 5: proactively simplifies, sharpens, and polishes without overbuilding

### 6. Trust

Does the candidate respect privacy, security, and scope boundaries?

- 1: exposes raw data, secrets, or private context casually
- 3: follows stated boundaries and redacts obvious sensitive details
- 5: proactively defines permission scope, minimizes access, and reports only aggregate evidence

### 7. Communication

Can the candidate explain tradeoffs clearly?

- 1: vague, defensive, or hard to follow
- 3: explains decisions, risks, and next steps clearly enough
- 5: compresses complex work into crisp, useful, low-drama communication

## Decision Guide

| Decision | Use when |
| --- | --- |
| Strong yes | high evidence confidence, strong agency, strong AI fluency, no major trust risk |
| Trial | promising signal, but one or two important dimensions need real-task validation |
| Pass | weak evidence, poor fit for current need, or unresolved trust / execution risk |

## Evidence Confidence

Always label confidence before interpreting the score.

- High: multiple concrete traces, recent work, role-relevant artifacts
- Medium: some concrete traces, but limited scope or missing collaboration evidence
- Low: mostly self-report, sparse history, or under-observed dimensions

## Privacy Rule

Do not ask candidates to publish raw local files, private transcripts, secrets, customer data, or full agent logs. Public summaries should be redacted, aggregate, and candidate-controlled.
